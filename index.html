The Bahamas

My first time outside of the United States was a family vaction to The Bahamas. We flew to Florida and then from there took a small plane to the island of Abaco. It was not a very touristy island which started my love of experiencing the more authentic areas of destinations. Some family friends had a vacation house on the island which we stayed at. It was gorgeously situated between a beach on one side and an ocean front on the other side. Everyone we met was so happy and kind. I think we all became enamoured with the island lifestyle. It was a very relaxing trip mainly spent outside enjoying the beaches and sights.

Ireland

My second international trip was to the country of Ireland. My mom and I flew to Dublin, Ireland to visit friends that were living there for six months on a sabbatical. Our friends were great tour guides and we packed in a lot of sights in the two weeks we were there. Within Dublin we visited Trinity College, St. Stephens Green, and The Temple Bar. We also went on bus tours to several locations across the country. We visited the Cliffs of Moher, Newgrange, and Avoca. Again, the people were great and it was a great experience that I will always cherish.

Greece

My favorite country I've visited is Greece. The trip was organized by my high school and I got to travel with fellow high school classmates. The lasted two weeks and we got to experience many locations in Greece. We started in Athens, went on a short cruise, and then ended the trip back in Athens. We visited the iconic city of Santorini with it's blue and white buildings. We visited the seaside town of Mykonos that had large windmills. My favorite city we visited was the ancient city of Olympia. Being able to stand in the same place where people stood many years ago was a fascinating experience.

Guatemala

My most recent international travel was to the country of Guatemala. I went with a group of young adults on a mission trip to help in the city of San Andres Itzapa. We spent a week there helping lay concrete floors in homes, build cinder stoves, and buld chicken coops. We stayed at a convent and had a great time getting to know the nuns who took great care of us. We had great guides that were members of the community. Towards the end of the trip we spent a fun day visiting Lake Atitlan and spent the afternoon hanging out on a boat. It was a very rewarding trip all around.
